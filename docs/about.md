## Professional Positioning

### As an AI Evaluator
I assess AI outputs against multi-dimensional rubrics covering accuracy, relevance, safety, coherence, completeness, and alignment with human values. I produce detailed written feedback that is directly usable in RLHF and RLAIF training pipelines. I understand the difference between a response that is merely plausible and one that is genuinely correct — and I can articulate that difference clearly.

### As a Prompt Engineer
I design prompts with deliberate intent: clear objective, scoped context, appropriate constraints, and testable success criteria. I iterate systematically, document results, and identify the structural patterns that cause models to fail or succeed. My prompt library spans general QA, creative generation, reasoning tasks, code explanation, and adversarial testing.

### As a Technical Writer
I produce documentation that people actually use. My writing is structured, direct, and precise — free of jargon where it doesn't belong and accurate where technical detail is required. I write for developers, end-users, and reviewers, adapting register and depth to the audience.

### As a Data Annotation Specialist
I annotate text with rigorous consistency, following complex multi-step guidelines without drift. I identify edge cases, surface ambiguities in annotation schemas, and flag content that falls outside established categories. I understand how annotation quality directly affects model behavior downstream.
